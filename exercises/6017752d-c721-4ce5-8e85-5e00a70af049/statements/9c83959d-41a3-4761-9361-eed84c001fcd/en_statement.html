Petrol

We are in crisis (that we are all tired of knowing) and to make even more unpleasant things, oil prices in the markets international markets continue to rise. Because of that, gasoline and diesel fuel are becoming more and more expensive. In fact, it seems that there is no week in that there is no increase in fuel prices. It won't be like that, but memory betrays us and so itâ€™s best to check.

Task

By consulting Galp's website, we have access to the price of gasoline, on each day since the beginning of the year, or, in general, since some date in the past. We intend, using this data, to dispel myths and calculate exactly the number of weeks that the price of gasoline has gone up at least one time.

Input

The first line has the number of weeks for which we have data about the price of gasoline. The second line has a number representing the price on the Sunday before the first week with complete records. For the purposes of this problem, we use the standard that states that the week starts on Monday and ends on the following Sunday. Following are so many lines as the number of weeks indicated in the first line, each with seven numbers, representing the price of gasoline on each day of the week. Gasoline prices are expressed by whole numbers in thousandths of euros. For example, the number 1759 means that the price is 1,759 euros.

Output

There is only one line where only one number appears: the number of weeks where the price went up at least once, from one day to the next. note that if the price goes up from the Sunday of the previous week to the Monday of the current week, counts as having risen in the current week.

two
