Hunt for Gambozinos

Gambozinos are described in some dictionaries as "fish or birds imaginary, with which, by way of a joke, they are able to achieve peace by sending them to hunting or fishing for these animals ", with burlap sacks so that they do not they escape, and perhaps with some tests to call or stun them.

Task

A program is intended to hunt gambozinos in a rectangular region given, divided into unit squares. This region will always have a border with a unit of width, all around, and that is empty. One gambozino consists of a set of five unit squares connected by edges (that is, it is a special polynomial), being able to be only of the five shapes shown in the figure, on the left. None another way that can be obtained from these, by symmetry or rotation, is gambozino. The program should analyze the region and indicate how many gambozinos found. During the search, gambozinos do not move. THE region may have other species of animals, equally immobile. It is known that gambozinos are not very sociable: they are isolated from each other others share at most vertices but never edges.

Input

In the first line there are two integers M and N that define the dimensions of the region, both greater than or equal to 5 and less than 50 units. Following are M lines describing the content of the region, supposedly divided into unit squares. Each line has N integers 0 or 1 separated by spaces, 0 indicating position empty and 1 occupied position. Animals, including gambozinos, are formed by 1 's contiguously horizontally and / or vertical. The edge of the region is always free as has been said, and, consequently, you will always have 0 's as in the examples presented below.

Output

A line with an integer (positive or zero) that represents the number of gambozinos found.
