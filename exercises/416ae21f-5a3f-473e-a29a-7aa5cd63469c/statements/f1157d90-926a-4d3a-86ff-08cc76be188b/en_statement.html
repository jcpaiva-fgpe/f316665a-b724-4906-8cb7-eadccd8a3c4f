Bart and the Mathematics

Our well-known Bart Simpson has a problem. He would love to buy a new skateboard, but his mother, Marge, will only do it if have good grades. But he has big problems with mathematics ...

Bart knows that in the next class he will be called to the board to factor out a number. Remember that a positive prime number is an integer positive greater than 1 which is only divisible by 1 and by itself. Factorizing a number is to break it down into a product of its prime factors. For example, 24 can be written as 2 * 2 * 2 * 3 (2 and 3 are prime) and 1050 can be written as 2 * 3 * 5 * 5 * 7 (2, 3, 5 and 7 are prime).

Bart could ask his sister Lisa for help, but he is too proud to that. Can you help him and show him how to factor a number?

Task

Write a program that, given a number, factores it, decomposing it into a product of prime factors.

Input

In the first line there is an integer C , which indicates the number of cases to be treated (1≤ C ≤20).
Follow exactly C lines, each containing a unique number integer N , which represents a number that we want to factorize (2≤ N <5000).

Output

It consists of C lines, each containing the factorization of a number (in the order they appear in the input , in the format NUMERO = FACTOR1 * FACTOR2 * ... * FACTORn . See the example to get a better understanding. You Prime factors must always come in ascending order of magnitude (that is, write 24 = 2 * 2 * 2 * 3 and never 24 = 3 * 2 * 2 * 2 ). Also note that there is only one and only one space before and after the symbol of equality.

Example of Input 

3
5
24
1050

Example of Output 

5 = 5
24 = 2 * 2 * 2 * 3
1050 = 2 * 3 * 5 * 5 * 7
