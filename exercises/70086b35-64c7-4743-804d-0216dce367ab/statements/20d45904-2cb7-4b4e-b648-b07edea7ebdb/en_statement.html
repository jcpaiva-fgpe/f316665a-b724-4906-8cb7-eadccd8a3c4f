Chinesices


Like the signs of the Zodiac, there are twelve Chinese signs. However, Chinese apply for a period of about a year and not about a year. month, as is the case with the Zodiac. Chinese signs are therefore order, rat , ox , tiger , rabbit , dragon , snake , horse , sheep , monkey , rooster , dog , and pig (intentionally without accents).

Chinese signs change in the Chinese lunar new year that does not coincide with the solar new year on the julian calendar so we rule in the west, but the difference is not big. For example, this year, when we we celebrated St. Valentine's Day - Valentine's Day - on the 14th of February, the Chinese welcomed the year of the tiger. Thus we can say with a slight error that the year 2010 is the year of the tiger.

Task

Given a year in the Julian calendar, we intend to obtain the Chinese sign corresponding, without accents. The years can vary between 5000 B.C. and 5000 D.C., the years of the common era being represented by positive integers and the years prior to the common era represented as negative numbers. It's from note that the Romans were unaware of the zero and as such there was never a year zero (which later gave rise to immense misunderstandings about whether the 2000 would be the beginning of the 21st century).

Input 

2010

Output 

tiger

2012
