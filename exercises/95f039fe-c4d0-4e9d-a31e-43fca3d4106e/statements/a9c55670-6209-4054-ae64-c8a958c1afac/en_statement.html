Price uncertain

João and his friends are creating an app to help save money on cost of motor fuels. At this point the app is already able to obtain the price per liter of fuel in all pumps located in a certain radius, as well as the distance they are. The problem is that the pump with the best price is often so far away that saving would dissipate in the cost of travel.

João's task is to determine the gas pump to recommend to the driver who uses the app, knowing how much they want to fill and which the average consumption of the car. For simplicity, it is assumed that after refuel, the driver returns to the starting point.

Task

Given the amount of gasoline you intend to supply and the consumption of car, and also a list of filling stations with indication of the its distance from the current point and the price per liter of fuel, determine the gas pump that allows a lower cost per liter effective (in cents, rounded by default).

The effective cost per liter also accounts for the cost of travel to the gas station (round trip). So, if you want to fill 50 liters but have to travel 5 kilometers to the pump, and another 5 to return, and the car uses 10 liters of gasoline for each 100 kilometers (that is, 1 liter for every 10 km) so, in the end, it was as if he had only 49 liters of the 50 he supplied. To the 49.5 liters actually available at the end, it is necessary to add the medium liter you spent on the way. If the price per liter charged at the pump is 1.30 euros would then pay 65 euros for the 50 liters, so the price per effective liter was 132 cents, which would correspond to paying 65 euros by 49 liters. In the estimate, it is assumed that the price of gasoline that you have in the tank before filling is equal to what you would pay. The cost per effective liter is obtained by rounding to the largest integer not higher (for example, 132.7 and 132.1 cents would be both rounded to 132 cents).

Input

The starting line contains two integers: the first is the quantity to be fuel (in liters) and the second the consumption of the car (liters for each 100 km). The second line contains an n integer that indicates the number of gas stations to be considered, being 1 . Each of the following n lines has two integers, referring to a pump: the distance it is (in meters) and the price per liter of fuel (in cents). It is assumed that this fuel is what the driver intends to use.


Note: Test cases will not be sensitive to loss of accuracy, being able to perform the intermediate calculations in floating point.

Output

The number of the pump to recommend, in the order in which they are listed in the data, with pump 1 being the first to be listed. If there is more than a pump with the best effective price , will be presented the one that find closer. If there is still more than one in these conditions, it will indicate which is first in the list.
