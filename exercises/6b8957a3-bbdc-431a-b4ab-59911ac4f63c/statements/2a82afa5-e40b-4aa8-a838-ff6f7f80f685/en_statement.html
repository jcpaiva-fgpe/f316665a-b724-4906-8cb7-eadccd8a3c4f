Problem G
Reservations for Maracanã Stadium

Problem

The Maracanã Stadium, located in the North Zone of Rio de Janeiro, was opened on 16 June 1950 and currently has a capacity for 200 000 people, which is reduced to 100 000 for security reasons. Assuming that the posts are numbered from 1 to 200 000, and that ticket reservations for places with these numbers, it is intended to verify whether a sequence of ticket reservations is acceptable . To this end, the total number of tickets sold should not exceed 100 000 and cannot be two (or more) tickets with the same number may be sold.

Task

Write a program that checks whether a sequence of reservations is acceptable and, if so, indicate the places sold, in a Synthesized. Reservations can be made for individual tickets or for multiple tickets with consecutive numbers.

Input

The sequence of reservations is given, one reservation on each line. The sequence ends with and always have at least one reservation and at maximum 100. Each reservation can be of the form or . In the first case, it is just a ticket, which must have the number . In the second, it's tickets whose numbers are those in the range , that is, , , ..., . For example, 
0 13
1 500 1000
1 57 499
...
would indicate, the reservation of number 13, of all numbers from 500 to 1000, of all from 57 to 499, ...

Output

You must indicate the total number of tickets booked, and on the lines following a representation of the set of reservations made.

The set must be ordered, and it will be the union of the sets that indicate on each line, and its representation must be the most compact possible. Whenever you have booked tickets with consecutive numbers, you should include them all in one interval. Isolated ticket reservations, should be grouped, as long as the final set is kept in order (as shown below).

The total number of tickets booked will be 0 if not possible to make the sequence of reservations, as this is not acceptable . In that case, you will not write anything on the next line.
