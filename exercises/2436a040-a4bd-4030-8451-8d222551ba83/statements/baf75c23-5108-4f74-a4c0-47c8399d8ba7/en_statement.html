Naval Battle

There was no Game Boy, PlayStation, New Nintendo 3DS, Wii, PADs, Smartphones, nothing, nothing ... paper and pencil was enough to give free rein to imagination and ensure a few hours of entertainment. We don't talk about Jurassic period. According to some records, the game Batalha Naval appears in World War I (1914-1918). It is, therefore, a game of bad memory but, however, centenary.

In the Naval Battle two players participate, each one with its fleet and the objective is to sink the opponent's fleet by guessing the position of the ships enemies.

Each player arranges his fleet in a grid and also maintains another one grid that represents the disposition of the opponent's fleet (according to what discovering it during the game). In positioning the fleet, you can choose any orientation for each ship, vertically or horizontally, provided that the ships do not touch each other at any point.

There are several versions of the game, which differ in the size of the grid, constitution of the fleet or in the rules of the game. Let's assume that the grid is \ (10 ​​\ times 10 \). In each game, the constitution of the fleet is the same for both the players, but we admit some flexibility in the type and number of ships to use. Possible types are: aircraft carriers ( P ), battleships ( E ), seaplanes ( H ), cruisers ( C ) and submarines ( S ). The figure shows their shapes. Players will decide how many to use of each type. How much to the rules, we assume that, each player fires three shots in each move and then it passes the turn to the opponent, unless his move is invalid. In response, the opponent tells you if the three shots hit the water or the number of shots that hit each type of ship hit (without saying how many ships it reached of each type).

Task

As paper-and-pencil and face-to-face games seem to be out of fashion, let's create a simple program to support the Naval Battle online . The program receives the disposal of each player's fleet and a sequence of moves and gives the feedback that the opponent would give to every move. The sequence ends if one of the players wins the game but can also finish at any stage of the game. The program must identify the chosen fleet.

Input

It starts with 10 lines, with 20 integers separated by spaces, which are 0 (water) or 1 (ship). The left part defines the positioning of player 1's fleet and the right defines the positioning of the player 2's fleet. Next there is a line with an integer positive \ (m \) representing the total number of moves. Then there's \ (m \) lines, each with three pairs of positive integers. Each pair corresponds to a shot and identifies the row and column of the position to be hit (rows and columns are numbered from 1, from top to bottom and from left to right). Always start player 1 . If any of the shots in a move refer to a position previously hit by the player (ship or water), this move is considered totally invalid and, on the next line, there will be a play by the same player.

Output

For each move, there is a line with the format " Player k: Water "," Player k: Invalid "," Player k: seq ", where k will be replaced by the identifier of the player and seq for information on the number of shots that hit each type of ship hit. Quotation marks are not included or accents. The sequence seq is a sequence of pairs \ (Tt \), with a space between each pair but not between \ (T \) and \ (t \), with \ (T \) being the identifier of a type of ship and \ (t \) the number of shots that hit ships of that type in this move (\ (t = 1,2,3 \)). The string should be ordered by type of ship (in the order P , E , H , C , S ).

If any of the players win the game, due to the destruction of the opponent, the output will have one more line with " Won the player k ", with k being the winner's identifier.
