ET in Shock

Problem

Yesterday, May 12th, he was found in the wonderful garden of the future building of the Department of Computer Science, a UFO with a life extra-terrestrial (ET) on board. The ET was removed from the ship without any physical injury, but in apparent shock. Called to the site, the INEM was unable to do anything to assess his condition, transporting him immediately to the nearest hospital.

Recognized Portuguese scientists then worked together to try to assess his health status. The discoveries were impressive. According to the clinical bulletin, released after several hours, they had managed to completely unravel the genetic code of ET. Your DNA was only made up of 3 distinct elements: c , t and a (the human genome consists of 4 elements). Scientists also realized that the DNA of ET was mutated by every hour that passed, following a constant pattern. The first element of the DNA was maintained but the remaining elements of the DNA were altered according to the following rule. For each pair of neighboring letters, the second it was changed if it was different from the previous one. In this case, it was replaced by the letter that was not in the pair. For example, for the DNA sequence actt , the mutation would be:

the first letter remains a
the second letter of the pair ac evolves into t
the second letter of the pair ct evolves into a
the second letter of the pair tt remains t
and the end result would be atat . Changes in a sequence occurred simultaneously.

Assignment

Write a program that, given an ET DNA status, calculates what will be your state after an hour.

Input

The input is a sequence of letters ( c , t or a separated by spaces), arbitrarily long, representing the DNA of the ET, and ending with # . In the data, there is one symbol per line. All lines have a newline character.

Output

The output is the DNA sequence after a mutation. It will end by line change.
