I don't like geometry

A circumference of center C and radius r is the place geometry of points on the plane that are r from the point C . The circle delimited by this circumference is made up by points whose distance C is less than or equal to r . THE circumference is the boundary of the circle and the remaining points constitute its interior.

If (a, b) and (c, d) are the coordinates of two points P and Q in the usual orthonormal Cartesian framework, then the square of the distance between P and Q is given by (c-a) (c-a) + (d-b) (d-b) .

Task

Write a program that given a circumference and a sequence of points characterize the position of each point in relation to the bounded circle by that circle, indicating whether it is inside , at the border or outside of the circle.

The first line of input has the coordinates of the center of the circle and the second has the radius measurement. In the next line you have the number of points of the sequence and, in the others, the x and y of each point. All values ​​are integers in the range [-1000,1000] (naturally the radius is positive).

The program presents the classification of points, in the order in which they were Dice. Each line of output follows the format (x,y ): pos , where pos is the point classification and x and y their coordinates.

Example

Input 


-1 0
10
10
6 100
-1 10
3 2
-3 2
7 6
10 -1
7 25
-1 0
8 5
25 -1000

Output 


(6,100): abroad
(-1,10): at the border
(3,2): inside
(-3,2): inside
(7.6): at the border
(10, -1): abroad
(7.25): abroad
(-1,0): inside
(8.5): abroad
(25, -1000): abroad
