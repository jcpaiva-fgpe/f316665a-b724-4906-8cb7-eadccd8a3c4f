Programming Tournament

Problem

The Department of Computer Science at the Faculty of Science of the University of Porto is promoting a programming tournament for students secondary: ToPAS.

The final classification is obtained by the number of problems solved for each team. In the event of a tie, the tiebreaker criterion is used total time spent. The total time spent by a team is the sum of the time spent on each problem solved by that team. The team that wins solve as many problems in the shortest time as possible.

Task

Write a program that, given the time spent by each team at solve each of the problems, determine which is the first, second and third place in the tournament. That is, order the teams according to the criteria already stated and select the three best classified. Assume that none of the three best ranked teams solves the same number of problems in the same total time.


Input

The first line of the input is four integers N (<100), P (<100), T (<600) and R (<10000) that indicate respectively the number of teams participating in the tournament, the number of existing problems, the time in minutes of the tournament and the number of problems resolved by all teams. The remaining R lines show information on the problems solved. Each line is a sequence of three integers, representing the number of the team, the number of the problem resolved and the time spent in minutes solving that problem.

Output

The output is three lines that present information respectively relative to first, second and third place in the tournament. Each line is a sequence of three integers, representing the number of the team, the number of problems solved and the total time spent.

Example of Input 


10 7 60 10
1 4 35
2 3 37
5 4 43
4 3 45
3 4 46
5 3 25
2 1 36
7 4 54
1 1 45
5 1 33

Example of Output 


5 3 101
2 2 73
1 2 80
