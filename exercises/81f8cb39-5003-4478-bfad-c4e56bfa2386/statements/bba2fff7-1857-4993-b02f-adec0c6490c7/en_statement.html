Final Combat

Problem

Two friends meet for a magic card tournament. Each has a deck of cards with monsters, each card containing an integer which indicates the character's combat strength. Each player stacks `` strategically '' letters (equal number for both players). The development of a game occurs in a sequence of fights as follows. Player A and Player B draw the card. top of your deck.

  • If player A's card is more powerful than player B's, then player A wins that match, and player B's card is for the player A. Both cards are placed at the bottom of player A's deck, with player B's card under player A's card.
  • If player B's card has more attack power than player B's card player A, then B wins that match. In this case, it is player B who remains with player A's card. Place your card on the base of your deck first letter and then A.
  • If both cards have the same attack power then a a tie. In this case, each player places his card on the basis of his cheap.
The game proceeds in a similar way. The particularity and interest of the game comes from the fact that the cards are magic. In such a way that when two cards meet, regardless of the outcome of the fight, the card power is decremented by one (because the monster gets tired of the fights and loses energy). If the value of the card reaches 0 it is withdrawn from game. This rule introduces interest in the game, because otherwise the player with the highest card I could never lose the game.

The player who loses all cards during a sequence of matches loses the game. To avoid very long games, if after neither player has won, the game is considered to be tied. If both players run out of cards simultaneously the game is also tied.

Assignment

Implement a program that, given the players' decks, simulates the game to determine which of the two players wins.

Input

In the first line is the number of cards they will play with (). In the following lines there is a description of the cards from player A's deck (ie integers that represent the power of the chosen cards, from top to bottom). Nas remaining, has the description of cards from player B.

Output

The program must write A , B or E , to indicate the player who wins the game, followed by a line change. Will write And if there was a tie.
