A company has a robotic warehouse: 2 robots place and remove the goods off the shelves without any human intervention. The warehouse is rectangular and each of the robots is assigned to one side. The game robots would never meet but, due to a design error, they can find yourself at a single point. At the center of the warehouse is the gas station battery charging that is common to both robots. The robots take the opportunity to go to this place when their batteries are low and close enough to the center of the warehouse. When they decide to load batteries simultaneously collide and human intervention is required.

The robots are controlled by instructions that assume that the warehouse is a regular grid of 30 by 15 cells, with the point of supply in the coordinates (0,0). A sequence of instructions is executed without cycles or jumps, indicating only the movements of the robot. The commands are just the integers 2, 4, 5, 6 and 8, representing the directions, as on the numeric keypad: 2 points south, 8 points North, 4 to the West and 6 to the East; 5 means staying in the same place (this command can be executed at any time). The sequence instructions is preceded by the location of the robot and terminated by 0. The robots are synchronized and every second execute a command, starting at exactly the same time. It is known that during the execution of the instructions the robot goes at most only once to the charging station of the batteries and stays there for a few seconds (executing command 5).

Task

It will be necessary to write a program that analyzes the sequences of commands from both robots and check for collisions; that is, whether the time intervals that the robots would be refueling were overlap. The program receives two sequences of instructions that have exactly the same length and already guarantee that the robots move in the warehouse interior. Each of them preceded by the robot's initial position and terminated by 0. If they lead to the collision of the robots, then the verification writes KO . If there is no danger of collision, write OK .
