Vacation Plan

People from the north who intended to do beach in the Algarve and Andalusia in the Easter holidays, was surprised by winds, downpours and a very cold. "Blancos llegaron y más blancos volvieron" , so already dream of a week or fifteen days of vacation that they plan to take in June, July, August or September, if the holiday allowance is not in the flash flood. It looks like it won't ... So a group of optimistic friends is already trying to choose a week of vacation, according to preferences and unavailability of each one. This week (seven days of vacation) has to start on a Monday and end on the following Sunday or, in Alternatively, start on a Saturday and end on the following Friday. Each group member indicated a sequence of periods that do not overlap and classified each period using integers: -1 to mark unavailability and values ​​from 1 to 5 for express preferences, with 5 being the maximum level. US periods that it did not include, it is also available but with level of zero preference, if friends think it is a good idea or if there is no alternative. The classification serves to score similarly each day of the referred period. The chosen week will be one that everyone can go to, after 1 June (Wednesday) and ending before 12 September 2011, and that has the maximum score, considering the sum of the scores accumulated in the seven days, after processing everyone's preferences. If there is more than one possible week, they will choose the next one, don't let the cold come back in force.

Task

A program is intended to process the information provided and indicate the date when the group will start the vacation (or detect the inconsistency if there is no possibility of going together).

Input

A first line with the number of group members. It remains the same number of blocks, each containing the preferences or unavailability of a member of the group. A block starts with a positive integer that denotes the number of periods the member referred to and then, in each row, has a quintet in the "day month day month preference" format, for example, 16 6 30 6 3 (which would represent the period from 16 June to 30 June with preference 3 on each day of that period; if it were 16 6 30 6 -1 , would indicate that the member could not go on that time course).

Output

If there is a week when everyone can, it will indicate the start date of holidays in the format: number of the day followed by a space followed by June , July , August or September . Otherwise, the word inconsistent .
