Crossfire


Problem

Consider a board with L lines and C columns. At A white ball is placed in one of the columns in the first row. shifts vertically along that column and one position at a time. In the remaining rows (in any column), balls may be placed black (at most one per line), representing projectiles. The balls black move only horizontally, one position at a time. The balls blacks move from a given initial column and according to a initial sense also given. When a black ball reaches the limit of the this board reverses the direction of its movement.


Task

Write a program that, given the dimensions of the initial L * C board, the column where the white ball is located, the number of black balls and, for each black ball, its initial position (row and column) and the direction of determine whether the white ball reaches the bottom of the board without collide with any of the moving black balls. In the event of a collision, the program should print Collide and the line corresponding to the ball black that collides with the white ball. Otherwise, the program must simply print that the ball Passes .


Input

The problem data is presented as follows:
  • a line with two integers L and C (<= 50) that correspond to the size of the board;

  • a line with a single integer ColBranca that indicates the column where the white ball is initially found;

  • a line with a single integer NBlack that indicates how many balls black will be used;

  • NBlack lines, each with three integers representing respectively: the line where the black ball will be placed, the column where it is initially and the direction -1 (for the left) or 1 (to the right) of the movement.


Output

The program should present as a result:
  • passes : if there is no collision.
  • collides n : if there is a collision, where n is the line number where is the black ball that collided with the white ball:
