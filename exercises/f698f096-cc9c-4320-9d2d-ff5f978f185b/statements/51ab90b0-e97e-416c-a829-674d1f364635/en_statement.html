Record of ups and downs of the walker

A walker likes to record the distance covered in each of the your walks. And, in order to control its evolution, you want to know the difference between the distances covered in consecutive walks.

Task

Write a program that, for each recorded walk (except the first), indicates if the distance covered in this walk has increased ( HIGH ), remained ( PATAMAR ) or decreased ( LOW ) in relation to the distance covered in the previous walk. When the distance has increased or decreased, the program calculates the difference between the distances covered, in kilometers and meters.

Input

The input has \ (n + 1 \) lines.

Each of the \ (n \) first lines has a record of a walk. THE line has two integers, \ (k \) and \ (m \), which indicate that the hiker covered \ (k \) kilometers and \ (m \) meters.

The last line, which no longer corresponds to a walk, has 0 0 .

Restrictions

\ (2 \ leq n \ leq 1 \, 000 \) Number of walks
\ (0 \ leq k \ leq 100 \) Kilometers covered in a hike
\ (0 \ leq m \ leq 999 \) Meters (in addition to the \ (k \) kilometers) traveled on a walking

Output

The output has \ (n-1 \) lines: one line for every two walks consecutive. The line corresponding to the walks \ (i \) and \ (i + 1 \) has the following format:
  • HIGH \ (k \) km \ (m \) m
    if the distance covered in the walk \ (i + 1 \) increased \ (k \) kilometers and \ (m \) meters in relation to the distance covered on the walk \ (i \);
  • PATAMAR
    if the distance covered in the walk \ (i + 1 \) was maintained at relation to the distance covered in the walk \ (i \);
  • LOW \ (k \) km \ (m \) m
    if the distance covered in the walk \ (i + 1 \) has decreased \ (k \) kilometers and \ (m \) meters in relation to the distance covered in walk \ (i \).

Example 1

Input 

10 200
9 800
10 250
20 0
20 0
0 900
0 0

Output 

DOWN 0 km 400 m
HIGH 0 km 450 m
HIGH 9 km 750 m
PATAMAR
DOWN 19 km 100 m

Example 2

Input 

1 205
1 205
0 0

Output 

PATAMAR
