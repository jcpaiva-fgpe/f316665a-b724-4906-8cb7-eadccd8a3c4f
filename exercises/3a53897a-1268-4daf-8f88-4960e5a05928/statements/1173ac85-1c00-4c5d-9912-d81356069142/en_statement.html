Eyjafjallajokull

The volcanic ash cloud from the Eyjafjallajokull volcano, which erupted on April 14, caused chaos in European airspace and forced the cancellation of more than one hundred thousand flights. Conditions favorable weather conditions allowed it to spread initially to southeast, at about 40 km / h. The forecast of its trajectory and dispersion was difficult because it depended on the evolution of the volcano's activity and the different wind patterns at various altitudes.

Task

The intention is to follow the trajectory of the cloud, starting from a position initial analysis, and to analyze how it affects certain European locations. The model was very simplified: the planisphere is described by a rectangular grid; each cell in the grid is a 2-sided square and is defined by the coordinates of its lower left corner, which belong to the range [-100000, 100000]; the cloud is a rectangle fitted to the grid, occupies one or more cells, and it is invariable (does not disperse).

It is assumed that the cloud always moves at the same altitude, and with speed constant in each unit interval. You can only move in the following directions / directions: N (0.1) , S (0, -1) , E (1.0) , O (-1,0) , SE (1, -1) , SO (-1, -1 ) , NO (-1,1) or NE (1,1) .

If a point on the cloud is at the position (X, Y) at a given time T and the cloud to move with speed (H, V) in the interval [T, T + 1 [, that point will be in position (X + H, Y + V) at time T + 1. During that interval of time, the cloud can pass through several locations, saying that they have been under the influence of the cloud in that period.

The sites are located in the center of the cells that contain them and are: Athens (12779, 20557) , Barcelona (1113, 22779) , Frankfurt (4445, 22779) , Funchal (-8887, 17779) , Lisbon (-4999, 21113) , London (1, 28335) , Madrid (-1665, 22223) , Paris (1113, 26667) , Porto (-4443, 22779) .

The total allocation level for each location will be given by the number of periods when the location had some point in the cloud over it. On each unitary interval, the level of affectation of the affected sites grows just one unit.

Input

In the first line there are four integers, A, B, C and D, separated by a space: (A, B) and (C, D) are the initial positions of the lower vertices left and top right of the cloud. In the next line you have the number of intervals to consider (maximum 300) and then, on each line, a pair of even integers H and V which gives the speed of the cloud in each these unit intervals. You can assume that the coordinates of the cloud are keeps in the range [-40000, 40000] during the simulation.

Output

In the first line you have the number of affected sites. If that number is zero, on the next line is " Did you go to the North Pole? ", without quotes or accents. If it is different from zero, in the following lines it has the alphabetical list of affected locations and levels of allocation corresponding names (the name will be separated from the value by a space).
