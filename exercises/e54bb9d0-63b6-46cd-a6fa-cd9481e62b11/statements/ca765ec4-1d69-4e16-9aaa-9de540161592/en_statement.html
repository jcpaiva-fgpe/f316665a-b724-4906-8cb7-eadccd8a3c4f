Stone, Paper, Scissors

In the game Rock, Paper, Scissors , two players form simultaneously one of three hand signals: the Stone is symbolized by a closed fist; the Paper, by the open hand; and the scissors, by two fingers stretched. Players compare the signals, to decide who won, from as follows:

  • the Stone wins the Scissors (kneading it);
  • the Scissors wins the Paper (cutting it);
  • the Paper wins the Stone (wrapping it).
If both players make the same gesture, a tie occurs. Usually the game is repeated until someone wins or by a number predetermined number of moves.

Task

Given the signal sequences of two players in 10 moves, it is intended to count the number of victories for each one. Each sequence is given in a line and consists of 10 letters of three types: R (Stone), P (Paper) or T (Scissors). The result should consist of two lines of text accounting for the number of first and second player wins.

Example 1

Input 

PTTTPPPPRP
RRTRPPRPTT

Output 

3
3

Example 2

Input 

TRPPRPTPRR
RPTTPTRTPP

Output 

0
10
