Soft water in hard stone



The proverb "soft water on hard stone, it beats until it sticks" does not want say someone is going to explode if they're continually under pressure, but yes, with persistence, difficulties can be overcome. It is natural that the effort required is greater in some cases than in others.

Task

Write a program that analyzes a sequence of \ (0 \) 's and \ (1 \)' s and counts how many times it was possible to overcome the difficulty level, considering various scenarios. A sequence of adjacent \ (1 \) 's represents a sequence of consecutive attempts and it is considered that if exceeded the level of difficulty of the scenario if the maximum number of \ (1 \) 's in a row exceed your difficulty level.

Input

A line with a sequence of \ (0 \) 's and \ (1 \)' s (without spaces). There follows a line with a positive integer \ (n \) representing the number of scenarios to analyze and \ (n \) lines, each with an integer positive indicating the level of difficulty of the corresponding scenario. THE sequence of \ (0 \) 's and \ (1 \)' s can be very large and \ (n \) too.

Restrictions

\ (1 \ leq t \ leq 100 \, 000 \) Maximum number of consecutive attempts in a sequence
\ (0 \ leq d <150 \, 000 \) Difficulty level of a scenario
\ (1 \ leq n <10 \, 000 \) Number of scenarios to analyze

Output

A line with a non-negative integer that indicates the number of scenarios where persistence (i.e., maximum number of consecutive \ (1 \) 's) was higher than the level of difficulty of the scenario.

Example

Input 

11110000110100011111000111110111101111111110111101111111101
6
two

7

Output 

4
