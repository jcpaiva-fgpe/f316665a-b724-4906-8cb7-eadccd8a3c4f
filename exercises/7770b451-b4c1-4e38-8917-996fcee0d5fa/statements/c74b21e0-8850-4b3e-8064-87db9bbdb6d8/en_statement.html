We feel


In some cinemas, theaters and sports venues there are no marked places. People are choosing places of their preference and, sometimes, they end up "giving a little way", moving to the chair of the side so that a group can sit in your queue. We are interested observe the dynamics of a row.

People arrive alone or in groups and present themselves on the left or on the right. For a group to feel, there must be places enough in the queue; if not, it will look for another queue, and it won't be much important for what follows. A group does not separate. The first person of the group occupies a place of their preference and the rest sit at their left if the group enters from the left, or to its right if it enters from the right. The place that the former intends to occupy is always free. If no there are places for everyone but the position immediately next to the occupied the first one is free, it moves in order to create places for the ones that are missing. If it is still not possible to seat everyone, then the group sits at one end (to avoid further confusion), occupying the innermost places. Preferably, sit in the extremity at which it is located, provided that this does not require the displacement of more people than you would need if you sat on the other end. In displacements, the relative positions of people seated previously remain, only people who are closer to the end where the group will be. For that, they occupy adjacent places that are free or that are free because other moving too.

No one will have to travel to make room for a person who is not in group. There will be no queue changes.

Task

Write a program to analyze a sequence of arrivals at a queue and indicate the final disposition of the groups in that queue. Groups are identified by the arrival order number (also count those who will have to look for another queue).

In the first line of input there is an integer (positive and less than or equal to 50) representing the number of seats in the queue. In the following lines there is a sequence of E N L integer suits where: AND is either 0 or 1 depending on the group presented on the left or right, N (greater than or equal to 1) is the number of elements in the group and L identifies the place where the first element intends. The number that identifies that place refers to end of the group (for example, 0 4 3 means that the first element wants the third position from the left, while in 1 4 3 it would be the third from the right). The scenario ends with 0 0 0 .

The output is the description of the final status of the queue, seen from left to right, one position on each line: free positions will have a dash; at occupied will have the identifier of the group to which the person belongs.
