Telepreso

Problem

The top head of an organized crime network is trapped in a chain of maximum security, in complete isolation, for 6 months. Even though he has managed to continue to manage the part of his organization that has not yet is behind bars because his associates sent him a mobile phone.

In reality, we realized this intention in time, but we chose for not preventing the cell phone from reaching you. We hoped to get from cell phone conversations information that would allow us to do other arrests and put him in a cell with ultra sensitive microphones. At the However, the prisoner communicates only by SMS and to telephone numbers on the foreign. To make the situation more difficult, messages are encrypted automatically by the phone itself and we are unable to decipher them.

Fortunately the microphones installed in the cell are sensitive enough to distinguish the tones of each key pressed. For we can quickly decipher the messages that the prisoner sends, we need a program that converts sequences of the same key in the associated letter. For example, three consecutive rings from the "8" key corresponds to the letter "V". Two more presses of the "3" key, followed by two touches on the "6" key, followed by two touches on the key "6", followed by a touch of the "3" key, followed by two touches of the key "3", form the word "SELL".

Note that the last two letters of the word "SELL" ("D" and "E") are achieved by sequences of the same key ("3"). If there is a certain delay between pressing two keys on mobile phones assumes that the sequence ended and a new one started.

Task

Convert a sequence of numeric values ​​to the letters that are assigned to them associated with the keypad of a mobile phone, according to the following table:

key Repeats
1 2 3 4
0 +
1 _
2 THE B Ç
3 D AND F
4 G H I
5 J K L
6 M N O
7 P Q R s
8 T U V
9 W X Y Z

Some keys can be repeated up to three times, two of them can be repeated four times and twice only once. To facilitate the legibility the "1" key is associated with the "_" character (`` underscore '') and not to the space character. You can assume that the keys are not repeated more times than the values ​​defined in the table. That is, you can assume that the "2" key is never pressed four times nor the "1" key twice or more times.

In addition to the numerical values ​​corresponding to the digits, also used two special numeric values:

  • 77 - represents a wait that allows us to consider the end of a sequence;
  • 99 - represents the end of the message.

Input

A sequence of lines, with an integer (from 0 to 9 or 77) per line, terminated by a line with the number 99.

Output

A line with the sequence of letters corresponding to the integers read.
