ToPAS parking

Usually, in the parking lots of shopping centers, parking spaces are arranged in parallel rows, two to two, with a circulation strip between each pair of rows. In the most sophisticated, there is a traffic light system to facilitate the location of free places by drivers who want to park. It's very simple: a traffic light is placed over each parking space. When the place is free, the traffic light turns green; when it is busy, it stays red.

The car park of the future TopaShopping in Loulé will use a revolutionary organization of posts, suppressing the bands of circulation. I mean, the parking plate will be divided into rectangular parking cells, all contiguous, each with a semaphore. It is true that, with disrespectful drivers, some cars they may be blocked, that is, unable to leave the park. Beyond addition, it may happen that certain areas of the park are empty but inaccessible, because there are parked cars surrounding this area completely. To make life easier for customers who want to park, helping them to find a place for the car, traffic lights will be used with three colors - green, red and yellow - working as follows:

At each moment, each of the parking cells will be occupied, free, blocking or isolated. Will be busy, with the red traffic light, if there is a parked car; will be free, with the green traffic light, if it is empty but not blocking or isolated; will be blocking, with the yellow traffic light, if empty, but if it was busy, at least one of the adjacent cells that are occupied would have the respective blocked car; will be isolated, with the traffic light off, if it is free but inaccessible from outside the park.

Note that a car can be blocked even without being trapped between four cars (one in front, one in the back, one on the left and one on the left right). Actually being blocked means that adjacent places ahead, behind, left and right are all occupied or isolated.

Also note that, since the car park is not fenced, cars parking in the end rows will never be blocked. Beyond In addition, you can enter the surface of the park by any of the parking cells at the end rows, provided they are empty. Soon, these cells will never be isolated.

Task

Please write a program that reads from the console the description of the the park and the occupied places and write on the console the plan of the traffic lights. The park is rectangular, of dimensions \ (W \) and \ (H \), < where \ (W \) is the measure of the horizontal side and \ (H \) is measured on the vertical side, and each parking space is represented by a pair of coordinates \ (x \), \ (y \), with \ (0 \ leq x 0 ; green, 1 ; red, 2 ; yellow, 3 .

Input

The first line of the input contains the values ​​of \ (W \) and \ (H \), in that order. The remaining lines, in an undetermined number, contain each a pair of coordinates \ (x \), \ (y \) of each place of parking that is currently occupied, without repetition. The end of the data is marked by a line containing the number -1 twice.

Restrictions

\ (1 \ leq W \ leq 100 \) Number of columns
\ (1 \ leq H \ leq 100 \) Number of lines

Output

The output is the matrix with the traffic light plane, written line by line, with all the numbers juxtaposed.
