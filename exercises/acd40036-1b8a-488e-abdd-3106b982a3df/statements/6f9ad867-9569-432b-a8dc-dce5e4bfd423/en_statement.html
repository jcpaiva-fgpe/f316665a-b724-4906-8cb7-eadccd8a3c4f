Sit or not Sit?

Problem

The inhabitants of the kingdom of Stubbornness are almost fixed in ideas, preferring stand up than sit in chairs for which they do not nurture special empathy. Only very exhausted are they seated in some who, not being one of their predilect, also do not totally reject. In society they respect a strict hierarchy, waiting for their superiors to feel or insist on standing up, to move on to a place of your own preferably still unoccupied.

Task

Write a program that, given the number of existing chairs in each type and given the inhabitants' preferences, in decreasing order of preference determines the number of those standing and the number of chairs that remain unoccupied. The inhabitants are exhausted.

Input

The first row has the number of types of chairs. The following are even data , where is a type and the number of chairs that exist of type . If there are no chairs of a given type , the data will have . Note that e are in consecutive lines.

After the information about the chairs, find the number of inhabitants of the kingdom and then the preferences of each, being already indicated according to the hierarchy of the kingdom . Each inhabitant is given the number of options (which can be ) and then the types of chairs in which you agree to sit, in descending order of preference.

There can be up to 100 types of chairs and up to 1000 of each type. Neither number of inhabitants of the kingdom nor the total number of seats exceeds 60000.

Output

Two lines: one with the number of inhabitants standing and the other with the number of chairs that are free. They end with a line change.
