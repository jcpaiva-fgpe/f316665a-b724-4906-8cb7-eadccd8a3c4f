Colored Lines

In the board game Color Lines , the objective is to line up five or more balls of the same color, as many times as you can, to score points. The board has a dimension . O alignment can be vertical, horizontal or any diagonal.

Normally the game starts with five balls on the board positioned at the chance. In each play, the player selects any ball and the position where you want to move it. The ball moves to that position as long as it be free and there is a free way there (that path is always a sequence of any horizontal and / or vertical unit steps). At new position, the ball may complete one or more alignments, each with 5 or more balls (as in the examples). Whenever this happens, the player accumulates points and the balls involved leave the board. Just leave balls belonging to an alignment of 5 or more balls. In the situation illustrated by the figure on the far right, would complete an alignment of 6 balls and one of 5 (and 10 balls would come out of the board).

When there is no alignment, new balls enter, occupying positions random. The game ends when the board runs out of space free.

It is only intended to know if the player is able to complete any alignment in a given play and, if successful, how many balls will come out of the board (before others enter).

Task

Write a program to simulate a move from a certain state and indicate how many balls leave the board in that move.

Each position on the board is identified by a pair (I, J) of integers between 1 and 9, where I is the row number and J is the column number: (1,1), (1,9), (9,9) and (9.1) are the bottom left, bottom right, top right and upper left, respectively. In each position there is a integer (from 1 to 7).

The input starts with the state of the board, given by lines, from the top to the base (0 means free position). The next two lines give the position the selected ball and the cell to move it to, if you can.

The output is a line with the number of balls that have come out (possibly 0).
