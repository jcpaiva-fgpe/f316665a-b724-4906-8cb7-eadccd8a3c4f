Encoding run-length

Run-length encoding is a technique for compressing text where there are long strings of repeated characters. For example, string AAAAAAA could be represented by 7A (seven times the A character), a fact that inspires representation that we're going to use.

In order to avoid ambiguity and not increase the length of the texts in the In the case of short sequences, we do not compress sequences with less than three repeated characters (i.e., they are unchanged). If a character is repeated three or more times, then it must be followed by a digit ( 0 to 9 ) which indicates the number of repeated characters (in addition to the initial three). If a character occurs repeated more than 12 times (3 + 9) then the rest must be treated as a new sequence; for example, AAA9AAA0 would be the encoding of AAAAAAAAAAAAAAA , that is, of the sequence of 15 (= 3 + 9 + 3 + 0) A characters.

Task
