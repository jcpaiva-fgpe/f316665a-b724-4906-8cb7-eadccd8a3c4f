Virtual Candy

How easy it is to distribute virtual candies! Just calculate the quotient and the rest of an entire division to find out how many candies you can give and how many are left. The biggest problem is sometimes remembering the multiplication table.

Task

Write a program to help analyze various scenarios, presenting the distribution result in each case.

In the first line of data you have the number of scenarios to consider. An equal number of lines follows, each containing two integers positive (number of sweets to be distributed and number of people) and two characters ( Q and R or R and Q ), separated by a space.

For each scenario, the program writes a line. If in the scenario you have Q followed by R , you will first have the quotient and then the rest, and if it's R and then Q , you'll have first the rest and then the quotient. In any case, you will always have to identify each value, preceding it by Q or R followed by a colon and a space.

Example

Input 

5
7 5 R Q
10 2 Q R
23 8 R Q
8 23 R Q
110 23 Q R

Output 

R: 2 Q: 1
Q: 5 R: 0
R: 7 Q: 2
R: 8 Q: 0
Q: 4 R: 18
