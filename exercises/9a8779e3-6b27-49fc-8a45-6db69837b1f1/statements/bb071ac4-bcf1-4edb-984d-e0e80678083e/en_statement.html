Sierpinski Triangle

Problem

Pascal's triangle is a possible representation for the coefficients of Newton's binomial expansion: (a + b) (n-1) The triangle can be defined using the rule: the order element on the line results from the sum of the order elements and da line . The triangle for is: 

1: 1

The Sierpinski triangle is obtained from the previous one following the same representation but replacing the odd elements with the character by character '*' and the pairs by the character '.'. For the triangle of Sierpinski is:

two:              *   *

4: * * * *
5: *. . . *
6: * *. . * *
