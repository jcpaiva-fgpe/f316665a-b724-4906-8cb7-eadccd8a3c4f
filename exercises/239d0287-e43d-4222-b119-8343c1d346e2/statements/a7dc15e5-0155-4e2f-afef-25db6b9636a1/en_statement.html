Rock in Rio


The crew of AirGlobe's planes receives a bonus whenever crosses the Greenwich meridian or the equator. The bonus can be converted into rest days and is expressed in points, attributed from the as follows: 1 point if the flight crosses the Greenwich meridian; two points if the flight crosses the equator; 4 points if the flight crosses the Greenwich meridian and the equator.

Commander Passarinho wants to go to Rock in Rio de Lisboa and needs to know if he already has the necessary points to be able to rest on those days. O Greenwich semimeridian, used as a reference for determining the longitude, is sometimes referred to as the `` Greenwich Meridian '', being normally represented on geographic maps (0 degree mark) longitude). However, Commander Passarinho also knows that the meridian Greenwich is a maximum circle that passes through the poles and divides the Earth in two hemispheres: the eastern hemisphere and the hemisphere western. He will not go around the world to earn more points, but he wants points you are entitled to, because meridian is meridian, it is not semimeridian!

Task

For the purpose of calculating the bonus, each flight is described by the coordinates geographical areas (latitude and longitude) of the departure airport and the airport arrival. Latitude, which measures the distance to the equator, is expressed in degrees and ranges from 0 to 90 degrees, north or south. The longitude, which measures the distance to the `` Greenwich meridian '', is also expressed in degrees and varies between 0 and 180 degrees, east or west.

With this information available for each of the flights flown by the Commander Passarinho, it will be easy to calculate how many points he has.

The data for the program is entered as follows: first, the number of flights of Commander Passarinho. Then there are so many lines how many the number of flights. In each line there are eight elements of information, in two groups of four. The first group describes the coordinates of the airport of departure and the second group describes the coordinates of the airport of arrival. In each of the groups, first comes an integer, with a value between 0 and 90 exclusive, then a letter capitalized N or S , then a number between 0 and 180 exclusive and then a capital letter E or W . In this description, the first pair of values, number and letter, represents the airport latitude, with N meaning northern hemisphere and S the southern hemisphere; the second pair represents the longitude of the airport, with E meaning that the airport is in the hemisphere eastern and W in the western.

The program will write a line, with an integer representing the number of points of Commander Passarinho.

Note that there is no airport located on the Greenwich meridian nor about the equator.

Example 1

Input 

4
41 N 8 W 38 N 9 W
33 N 7 W 68 N 95 E
1 S 69 W 3 N 25 W
48 N 175 E 48 N 175 W

Output 

4

Example 2

Input 

5
81 N 120 W 12 S 11 W
9 S 5 E 33 N 155 W
34 N 56 E 68 N 95 E
2 S 2 W 9 N 42 W
48 N 6 E 38 N 9 W

Output 

9
