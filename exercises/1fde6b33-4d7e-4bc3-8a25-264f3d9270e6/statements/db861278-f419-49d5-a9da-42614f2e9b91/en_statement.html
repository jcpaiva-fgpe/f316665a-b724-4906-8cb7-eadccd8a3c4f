Agenda issues

People with overloaded schedules have some difficulty in scheduling meetings. There are those that say "I can from x to y , but to y I already have another meeting "and the ones that say" I can't z at t , but at t I'm already free ". The others possibilities are not going to be interesting right now and for simplicity's sake let's suppose they always say "I can".

Task

Write a program that, given the expected duration (in hours) for a meeting between two people and two pairs of integers A B and C D , check if it is possible to schedule the meeting and when. In this case, the first person would say "I can from A hours to B hours, but at B I already have another meeting "and the second would say" I can C hours at D hours, but at D I already have another meeting ", with A, B, C and D integers between 9 and 19. A is assumed to be less than B and C is less than D .

The meeting must take place between 9 and 19 hours and its duration is a whole. The first line of data has this duration and the next two the two pairs mentioned. Depending on the case, the program writes a line or with the word Impossible (without accent) or with an integer if the meeting only if it can be held in an hour or with two integers if there is flexibility (these integers define the interval in which you can fix the beginning of the meeting).

Example 1

Input 

1
9 12
12 16

Output 

Impossible

Example 2

Input 

two
10 15
10 17

Output 

10 13
