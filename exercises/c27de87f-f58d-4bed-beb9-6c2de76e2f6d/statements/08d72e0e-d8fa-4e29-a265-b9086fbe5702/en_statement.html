Saturday afternoon fever


Searching the attic we find a true relic: one of the first digital thermometers of the last century that, in addition to the temperature current, it also displays the maximum and minimum temperatures of the last 24 hours. Unfortunately the thermometer is out of order and there does not seem to be a way to calibrate it.

However, as we know the maximum and minimum temperatures recorded on the day above, and we can easily know what the maximum and maximum temperatures were minimum effective consultations with a newspaper, we can apply a linear function to correct the thermometer values. That is, to correct the values we can use a function whose graph is a line.

The correction function will transform the minimum temperature of the thermometer into actual minimum temperature and the maximum temperature of the thermometer at the temperature actual maximum. We can assume that the minimum and maximum temperatures recorded by the thermometer they are never of equal value.

Task

Your task is to make a program that starts by reading the temperatures minimum and maximum temperatures recorded by the thermometer, then the minimum and maximum temperatures, followed by a sequence of temperatures terminated by a value equal to or greater than 100.0. For each of these temperatures (except the four initials used to calibrate and the one that ends the sequence) the program writes the temperature value applying the correction linear. The values ​​for all temperatures are floating point, and the values ​​must be rounded to one decimal place.

Example of input 

0.0 30.0
10.0 20.0
0.0
30.0
15.0
10.0
100.0

Example of output 

10.0
20.0
15.0
13.3
