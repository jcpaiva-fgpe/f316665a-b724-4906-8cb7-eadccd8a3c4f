But what a heat!

Everyone agrees that the climate is changing. Some think that days are hotter, others guarantee that they are the nights that no longer are so cold, others that are the thermal amplitudes (the difference between consecutive maximum and minimum temperature) that have increased, or perhaps the opposite, now the temperatures cannot be distinguished. To take off this clean we will make a program that determines all these maximums, minimums and variations.

Task

Write a program that calculates the maximum, the minimum, the maximum amplitude and the minimum amplitude of a sequence of temperature values. At temperatures are given by integers in degrees centigrade. THE amplitude is the difference between a local maximum and a local minimum consecutive. A value is a local maximum if it is greater than or equal to your neighbors on both sides. Likewise, a value is a local minimum if is less than or equal to its neighbors. Note that the maximum or minimum can be maintained throughout consecutive readings, as it happens in the example with the minimum location where the maximum amplitude is reached. We can assume that the initial and final values ​​of the given series are always a local minimum or maximum. We also assume that there are no three records consecutive consecutive periods.
