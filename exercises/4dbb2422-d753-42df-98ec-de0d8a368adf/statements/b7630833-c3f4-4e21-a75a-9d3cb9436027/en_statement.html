Areas and Perimeters

Problem

A rectangle is a quadrilateral whose internal angles are straight. O perimeter of a rectangle can be calculated by adding the length of the your sides. The area is given by the product of the lengths of two sides adjacent. The square is a rectangle whose sides are all the same.

Task

Make a program that, given the size of two adjacent sides of a rectangle, calculate its area and its perimeter. When writing the result indicates whether it is a square or not.

Input

A line containing two non-negative integers (not more than 1000) representing the length of two adjacent sides (expressed in meters).

Output

Two sentences are written, as indicated in the examples.

Example 1

Input 


5 5

Output 


The perimeter of the square is 20 m.
The area of ​​the square is 25 m2.

Example 2

Input 


10 5

Output 


The perimeter of the rectangle is 30 m.
The area of ​​the rectangle is 50 m2.
