Silly Cicadas

Problem

Fed up with hearing that they spent the day singing, some cicadas decided to imitate their rivals. Not really understanding the logic of displacement on paths, we went to find them stunned: having left a certain place in single file, began to walk around, systematically passing through places where they had previously been, as if they were lost in a maze. They finally found the place they intended to reach. In anyway, we would like to know what they could have done if they had had a better sense of orientation and had not `` visited '' the same locations repeatedly.

Assignment

Write a program that analyzes the sequence of places that have been `` visiting '', which includes the point of departure and the end of arrival, and find a route that respects that sequence but removes all turns unnecessary. Only the last pass at each of these locations can be considered useful. All others are deviations. In case of mistake, all the rounds that cicadas gave, since they passed in a given place until who returned there, will be considered unnecessary. The place of arrival was not visited twice, but any of the rest, including the origin, it may have been.

Input

It is only given the sequence of places where the cicadas passed by, one location per line of data. The last line has a value of 0. The number of distinct locations does not exceed 30. Each location is identified by an integer positive value less than 10000.

Output

The sequence of places they would visit if they had not been mistaken, each location on a line.

15
2315

1315
0

Output 


1600
15
1315
