Problem D
Tetris simulator

Problem

Who doesn't know the famous Tetris game? When in the years 1985 and 1986 Alexey Pajitnov and Dmitry Pavlovsky, two programmers at the Center for Computing of the Russian Academy of Sciences, invented the game, inspired in the idea of ​​pieces falling into a glass cup, they never imagined success that this one would come to have. And to think that the first version of the game had only 4 colors and a resolution of 320x200!

Task

Your task is to create a program to simulate a game of Tetris. You can assume that, as illustrated in the figure, the game is always a checkered matrix of 15 rows by 8 columns. At pieces are small sets of connected squares that fall with the force of the gravity, stopping when any of its parts touches another part or at the end of the board. When a line is completely filled, disappears, and all the lines above it fall, moving one line down. For each completed line, the player wins one series of points and goes up in level, with the pieces falling each time faster. but this final part doesn’t interest you and what you are asked for is a program that, given a sequence of parts, as well as the where they fall, tell us how the final board configuration looks. Initially, the board is always completely empty.

Input

The first line of input has a number () which tells you the number of pieces in the sequence, followed by lines describing the pieces, in order of fall. Each piece is described in a line with 3 numbers: , and (, ; ) indicating the number of lines and columns of the piece and the leftmost column number of the piece, which indicates where the piece falls. These 3 numbers are followed by lines, each one with characters, indicating the configuration of the part. A '#' means a filled square and a '.' means a space in White.

Output

The output consists of 15 lines, each with 8 characters, indicating the final configuration of the board. As in the input, a '#' means a filled square and a '.' means a blank space.
