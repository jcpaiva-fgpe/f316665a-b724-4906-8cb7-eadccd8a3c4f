Minute Green

More than half of the waste we produce every day can be recycled. Therefore, citizens must actively participate in the correct separation waste for later recycling.

Cardboard - blue container (products 1 to 10), paper and cardboard, newspapers, magazines and writing paper;
Packaging - yellow container (products 24 to 40), packaging of plastic and metal;
Glass - green container (products 11 to 23), glass containers;

Task

Given a sequence of products to recycle, identified by numbers 1 to 40, determine how many would be placed in each container.

Input

A sequence of integers (with values ​​from 1 to 40) and ending with -1. The number of values ​​for each type is less than 2 ^ 30 = 1GByte.

Output

In each line write a sequence color: number , in which color is the color of the container and number is the number of products placed in that container. It starts with the blue colored container, then yellow, and finally green.

Example 1

Input 


3
13
13
-1

Ouput 


blue: 1
yellow: 0
green: 2
