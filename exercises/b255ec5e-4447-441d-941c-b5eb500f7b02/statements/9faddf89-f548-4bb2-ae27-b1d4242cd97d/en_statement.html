The Traveling Baker

João studies Computer Science and also works in a bakery for help support the costs of studies. He delivers bread every mornings to different bakeries and pastry shops in Espinho. Pray for hinder your work, even when you have to go to the same places you can having to do it in a different order. So, with some classmates he decided to develop a GPS guide home made . When they picked up the map João's distribution zone (given in the figure), they soon realized that the problem was facilitated by being in Espinho: the streets formed a pattern almost square and their names were numbers. Fruit of good organization, decided to divide the work by several components. It was up to João develop the part that gave the directions knowing the points where it was going pass.

Task

João leaves every day from the central bakery, point (4,5) , and his van is always facing East (on the map, to the right). For the sequence of journeys that he has to take that day, the GPS home made goes on giving the directions to follow, saying if it will move on ( F ) or if you have to turn left ( E ), turn right ( D ), or reverse ( I ). If it is not necessary to change of direction says forward ( F ). So, when there is an intersection it gives always an indication, as well as at the beginning of each route. If you give E , D or I , no longer gives F . When arrives at the end of a route, that is, to a customer, says stop ( S ).

Each route starts at the place where João is at the moment and is described by a sequence of dots that identify all intersections where you must change direction. The last point on a route defines a delivery location (customer).

Input

In the first line you have the number of customers you should visit (in the maximum 4), which corresponds to the number of journeys to be made. It follows the description of the routes, which, for each route, begins with an integer which indicates the number of points that define it, and then has the coordinates of those points. Each point is given on a line by two integers separated by a space.

Output

On each line, the sequence of directions to follow on each route, identified by the letters F , E , D , I , and S , as described above.
